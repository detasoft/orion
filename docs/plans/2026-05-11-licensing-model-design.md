# Licensing Model Design

## Goal

Adopt a source-available licensing model that permits free internal
self-hosting, keeps commercial SaaS and paid support under DETA PRO B.V.
control, and guarantees that each version later becomes open source under
AGPLv3-or-later.

## Decision

Use the Business Source License 1.1 for current versions and set the Change
License to GNU Affero General Public License version 3 or later.

Project-specific BSL parameters:

- Licensor: DETA PRO B.V.
- Change License: AGPL-3.0-or-later.
- Change Date: four years after first public distribution of each version.
- Additional Use Grant: allow production use for internal self-hosted business
  operations, but require a commercial agreement for hosted access, SaaS,
  managed services, resale, embedding in a commercial product or service, paid
  support, paid maintenance, paid operations, or paid professional services
  where Orion is a material part of the offering.

## Contributor Model

Contributors keep copyright in their contributions. Contributions are accepted
only with a CLA that grants DETA PRO B.V. broad rights to use, sublicense, and
relicense contributions, including commercial licensing. This preserves the
project's ability to make commercial agreements while keeping accepted changes
available in Orion under the project license and later AGPLv3-or-later.

## Trademark Model

Keep trademark rights separate from source-code rights. Forks may exist under
the software license, but Orion, DETA, and DETA PRO branding cannot be used for
unofficial products or commercial services in a way that implies endorsement.
